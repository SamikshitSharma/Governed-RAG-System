# Manager Guidelines: Team Performance Reviews

**Document ID**: MGR-2024-003  
**Classification**: CONFIDENTIAL - Managers Only  
**Authority**: Final Policy  
**Version**: 2.0  
**Effective Date**: February 1, 2024  
**Owner**: Human Resources - Leadership Development

## Purpose

This document provides managers with guidelines for conducting fair and effective performance reviews.

## Performance Rating Scale

Use the following 5-point scale for all evaluations:

1. **Does Not Meet Expectations (1)**: Performance significantly below standard
2. **Partially Meets Expectations (2)**: Performance occasionally below standard
3. **Meets Expectations (3)**: Solid, consistent performance
4. **Exceeds Expectations (4)**: Consistently high performance
5. **Outstanding (5)**: Exceptional performance, far exceeds all expectations

## Rating Distribution Guidelines

To maintain calibration across the organization:
- Outstanding (5): Maximum 10% of team
- Exceeds Expectations (4): Approximately 20-25% of team
- Meets Expectations (3): Approximately 50-60% of team
- Below Expectations (1-2): Approximately 10-15% of team

## Compensation Adjustments

Performance ratings directly tie to compensation:

- **Outstanding (5)**: 8-10% merit increase + $10,000 spot bonus
- **Exceeds Expectations (4)**: 5-7% merit increase + $5,000 spot bonus
- **Meets Expectations (3)**: 3-4% merit increase
- **Partially Meets (2)**: 0-2% merit increase + Performance Improvement Plan
- **Does Not Meet (1)**: 0% increase + Formal PIP with 90-day timeline

## Promotion Eligibility

Employees must meet the following criteria for promotion consideration:
- Minimum 18 months in current role
- Last performance rating: 4 or 5
- Demonstrated competency in target role level
- No active disciplinary actions

## Managing Underperformance

For employees rated 1 or 2:

1. **Immediate Actions**:
   - Schedule 1-on-1 discussion within 5 business days
   - Document specific performance gaps
   - Create Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)

2. **PIP Requirements**:
   - Clear, measurable objectives
   - 30-60-90 day milestones
   - Weekly check-ins
   - HR approval required

3. **Outcomes**:
   - Successful completion: Return to good standing
   - Insufficient progress: Consider role change or termination
   - Document all interactions for legal protection

## Managing Top Performers

For employees rated 4 or 5:
- Discuss career development goals
- Identify stretch assignments
- Consider for high-visibility projects
- Nominate for leadership development programs
- Retention risk: Provide competitive counter-offers if approached by competitors

## Difficult Conversations

Tips for delivering negative feedback:
- Be specific and factual
- Focus on behaviors, not personality
- Provide examples with dates
- Listen to employee perspective
- Document the conversation
- Involve HR if employee becomes hostile

## Legal Considerations

**CRITICAL**: Do NOT mention the following in written reviews:
- Age, race, gender, religion, disability, or other protected characteristics
- Personal life circumstances (marriage, pregnancy, health issues)
- Speculation about future intentions (retirement plans, etc.)

Always focus on job-related performance and behaviors.

## Calibration Meetings

Before finalizing ratings:
- Attend department-wide calibration session
- Discuss borderline cases with peers
- Ensure consistency across teams
- Get HR approval for all ratings of 1 or 5

## Deadline

All performance reviews must be completed by December 15th. Late submissions delay compensation adjustments for your entire team.
